Most retail "research" is a story told after the fact. Systematic investing flips
that: the rules are fixed in advance, the universe is known, and every position is
a consequence of the model — not a hunch.

## Rules over discretion

A rule you can write down is a rule you can test. Once a strategy is expressed as
code over a known universe, three things become possible:

- **Backtesting** against decades of history, honestly out-of-sample.
- **Attribution** — you know *why* a position exists.
- **Repeatability** — the same inputs always produce the same trades.

> Discretion hides its mistakes. A rule files them in plain sight.

## Showing the signals

We publish the *shape* of every strategy — its description, universe, and historical
curve — on a public page. The **live signals** and the **real book** sit behind a
sign-in, modelled as entitlements so a paid tier can come later. The point isn't to
gate knowledge; it's to separate the educational surface from the operational one.
